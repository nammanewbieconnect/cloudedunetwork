Overview of CISCO Training

This CISCO certification course is designed to provide learners with the skills and knowledge required to install, configure, operate, and troubleshoot medium-size routed and switched networks. The course covers key concepts such as network fundamentals, IP addressing, routing and switching, security basics, and automation. It prepares students for various CISCO certifications like CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate), CCNP (Cisco Certified Network Professional), and more.

Benefits of learning

Industry Recognition: CISCO certifications are globally recognized and respected in the IT industry. They serve as proof of your knowledge and expertise in networking technologies. Career Growth: CISCO certification holders often command higher salaries and are more likely to get promoted to senior roles in network administration, security, and engineering. Job Security: With the increasing demand for networking professionals, holding a CISCO certification makes you more valuable to employers, offering greater job security and mobility.

Why should you learn

  • High Demand for Networking Professionals: As businesses become increasingly reliant on IT infrastructure, the demand for skilled network professionals continues to grow. Comprehensive Skill Development: Learning CISCO provides you with in-depth knowledge of networking, security, and automation technologies, making you a versatile IT professional. Wide Range of Career Opportunities: CISCO certifications open doors to various roles such as Network Engineer, Systems Administrator, IT Manager, and more. Path to Specialization: Once you achieve a CISCO certification, you can specialize in areas like security, cloud computing, or data centers, enhancing your expertise and career prospects.

CISCO Training Course Syllabus

Module1- Networking Basics

  1. Introduction to networking concepts: LAN, WAN, VLAN, VPN
  2. Understanding network devices: routers, switches, firewalls, and access points
  3. Overview of network topologies, protocols, and OSI models.

Module 2- IP Addressing and Subnetting

  1. Understanding IP addressing (IPv4 and IPv6).
  2. Subnetting techniques and IP address management
  3. Configuring static and dynamic IP addressing

Module 3 -Routing and Switching Fundamentals

  1. Introduction to routing protocols (RIP, OSPF, EIGRP).
  2. Configuring and troubleshooting routing tables
  3. Understanding switching concepts, VLANs, and trunking

Module 4-CISCO Device Configuration

  1. Setting up CISCO routers and switches.
  2. Configuring basic device settings: IP addressing, interfaces, and routing protocols
  3. Implementing security features such as access control lists (ACLs)

Module 5 - Network Security Essentials

  1. Overview of cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities
  2. Configuring firewalls and intrusion prevention systems (IPS)
  3. Implementing secure network designs and VPNs

Module 6 - Wireless Networking

  1. Introduction to wireless networking principles.
  2. Configuring CISCO wireless devices and protocols
  3. Troubleshooting wireless connectivity and performance issues

Module 7 -Network Automation and Programmability

  1. Introduction to network automation using Python and CISCO DevNet.
  2. Configuring automation tools for network management and monitoring
  3. Understanding APIs and network programmability

Module 8 - Exam Preparation

  1. IPractice exams and questions.
  2. Study tips and strategies for passing the CISCO certification exam
  3. Hands-on lab exercises to reinforce key concepts
